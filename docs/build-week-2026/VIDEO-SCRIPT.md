# Build Week Demo Video Script

Target length: **2 minutes 40 seconds**. Do not exceed three minutes. Record the public site at https://hiri-protocol.org/ with audible narration and no copyrighted music.

## 0:00-0:15 - Title and honest boundary

**Screen:** Open the public demo on the Passport page with the Synthetic Demo ribbon visible.

**Narration:**

> This is HIRI Passport, a mobile-first exploration of holder-controlled digital credentials. This Build Week project is a working Synthetic Demo: it uses generated data and creates no real keys or credentials.

## 0:15-0:40 - The problem

**Screen:** Show the sample credential list, then open Professional Engineer.

**Narration:**

> Digital credential interfaces often collapse several different questions into one trust badge. HIRI Passport keeps cryptography, credential status, issuer identity, provenance, and relying-party policy separate, because a valid signature alone does not answer the other questions.

## 0:40-1:10 - Credential evidence

**Screen:** Scroll through the independent evidence results and inspectable hashes/schema information.

**Narration:**

> Here the holder can see what the sample credential claims and inspect each evidence dimension independently. Unknown or not-evaluated evidence stays visible instead of being promoted to success.

## 1:10-1:40 - Consent flow

**Screen:** Open Present, select Load synthetic consent preview, then Inspect request. Show the warning, requested field, purpose, and Decline request button.

**Narration:**

> A disclosure request enters through an explicit local action. The holder sees who is asking, what field is requested, why it is requested, and whether the identity is actually known. Decline remains a first-class choice.

## 1:40-2:05 - Mobile product quality

**Screen:** Narrow the browser or show a phone recording. Briefly open Privacy history or Settings.

**Narration:**

> The experience is mobile first, keyboard and accessibility tested, local in its normal demo flow, and backed by Core, PWA, browser, privacy, offline, update, and hostile-input tests.

## 2:05-2:35 - Codex and GPT-5.6

**Screen:** Show the repository, plan files, tests, and green GitHub Actions checks.

**Narration:**

> I supplied the HIRI project direction and made the product, risk, and governance decisions. Codex with GPT-5.6 Sol reviewed and harmonized the specifications, converted them into dependency-ordered implementation contracts, built the application module by module, wrote and ran the tests, corrected failures, and prepared exact review evidence. It accelerated the path from specification to tested software while leaving independent human approval explicitly human.

## 2:35-2:40 - Close

**Screen:** Return to the demo home with the project URL visible.

**Narration:**

> HIRI Passport is live at hiri-protocol.org, with the complete MIT-licensed source and testing instructions on GitHub.

## Recording checklist

- Keep the Synthetic Demo label visible early in the video.
- Show the working public URL, not a design mockup.
- Mention both Codex and GPT-5.6 aloud.
- Do not call the project production ready, independently reviewed, certified, or conformant.
- Do not display passphrases, tokens, private files, notifications, email, or personal browser data.
- Upload publicly to YouTube and verify playback while signed out.
